# Biological Neurons

### Example: Intersecting Hyperplanes in 2D

![[Pasted image 20260316030759.png]]
> Reference: Slide 31 - A 2D space partitioned by three intersecting lines ($g_1, g_2, g_3$). The resulting regions are labeled with 3-bit binary codes (e.g., 000, 001, 111) corresponding to vertices of a 3D hypercube.

* regions defined by hyperplane intersections correspond to hypercube vertex
* The first layer of neurons divides the input space into polyhedra formed by hyperplane intersections.
* Vectors located inside a region are mapped onto a hypercube vertex.
