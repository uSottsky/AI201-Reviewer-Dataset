# Biological Neurons

![[Pasted image 20260316025545.png]]
> Reference: Slide 3 - Detailed diagram of a Synapse showing the Sending neuron releasing Neurotransmitters from vesicles into the synaptic cleft, which then bind to Receptors on the Receiving neuron to trigger a cellular response. Also shows Transporters and Degradation/Metabolizing enzymes.

**Synapse:** the junction point of two neurons across which a nerve impulse passes
* when a nerve impulse reaches the presynaptic junction, neurotransmitters are released. Examples of neurotransmitters: acetylcholine, GABA, dopamine, serotonin.
* Neurotransmitters diffuse across the synaptic junction to enhance or inhibit (depending on the type of synapse and neurotransmitter), the receiving neuron's own tendency to emit electrical impulses.
