# Compact vs Noncompact Classes

![[Pasted image 20260315204741.png]]
> Reference: Slide 28 - (a) Two small compact circular distributions separated by a decision line. (b) Two large overlapping circular distributions where a shift in the decision line has a massive impact.

* **For compact classes** ($\sigma^{2}$ is small with respect to $||\mu_{i}-\mu_{j}||$), the location of the hyperplane is insensitive to the values of $P(\omega_{i})$ and $P(\omega_{j})$.
* **For noncompact classes** (variance is large), a small movement of the hyperplane may be critical.
