# Properties of Environments

### 6. Discrete vs Continuous
An environment is discrete when there is a limited number of distinct, clearly defined percepts and actions.

An agent should be designed to work for a whole set of different environments (called environment class).
* a chess program could be designed to take advantage of specific weakness of an opponent and may not be suited for a tournament.
