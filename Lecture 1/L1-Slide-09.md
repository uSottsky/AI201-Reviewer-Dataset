# Definitions of AI

Perfect Rationality (always doing the right thing all the time) is not possible in complicated environments because the computational demands are just too high

### Bounded Rationality or Limited Rationality (H. Simon, 1957)
economic theory that consumers have limited rational decision making, driven by three main factors - cognitive ability, time constraint, and imperfect information driven to make suboptimal decisions by emotions or moods

* **satisficing** - decision-making strategy that aims for a satisfactory or adequate result, rather than the optimal solution

### Satisficing Behavior
* acting under bounds of time and space
* instead of maximum effort toward attaining the ideal outcome, focus on good enough (satisfactory) solutions
