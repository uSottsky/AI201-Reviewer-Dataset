# Five Laws of Intelligent Action

### The Five Laws of Intelligent Action
5. Knowledge Compensates for Lack of Search
Knowledge reduces uncertainty and constrains the exponential growth of search needed to solve many problems. Combinatorial explosion is avoided using knowledge.
* e.g. Rubik's cube: most people will take half an hour or more to solve this puzzle but the time is reduced dramatically with practice

Importance of knowledge in speech:
* Sphinx: turn off syntactic knowledge source and sentences of the form "Sleep roses dangerously young colorless" become legal (error rate increases from 4% to 30%). Remove the probabilistic knowledge about frequency of word occurrences and error rate increases to only 6%.

Any-time Algorithm - can be interrupted anytime and will return results whose value monotonically increases with time
Any-space Algorithm - can work with arbitrarily low memories and guarantee optimal solutions upon termination
