# AI Problem Domains

### Speech Recognition
* speech recognition is several orders of magnitude more complex than tasks with low data rates and/or without time constraints
* usually operate in real time; must exploit vast amounts of knowledge; must tolerate error and imprecision; must use language; must learn from examples
* provide insights into the structure of intelligent systems, such as how to use incomplete, inaccurate, partial knowledge in problem solving
* 1970's: Harpy, Hearsay, HWIM (hear what I mean) connected speech systems used syntax and semantics as major knowledge sources
* Sphinx III (CMU) - 50,000 word vocabulary on a voice-mail dictation task; near real time performance on a Pentium Pro PC; speaker-independent
* 2002: DARPA EARS Program - keyword spotting in telephone conversations
* 2007: Connectionist Temporal Classification
* 2015: Google Voice - dramatic performance jump of 49% using LSTM

Speech recognition led to basic techniques such as blackboard models, network representation, Hidden Markov Model-based learning, beam search, and CTC
