# Five Laws of Intelligent Action

### The Five Laws of Intelligent Action
4. Search Compensates for Lack of Knowledge
Search is trial-and-error behavior. When faced with a puzzle we have never seen before, we engage in trial-and-error until a solution is found.
* In the 1960's and 70's, it was believed that masters-level performance in chess cannot be achieved except by codifying and using the knowledge of expert human chess players;
* Deep Blue's knowledge database is very small compared to a chess master (just opening and end games are encoded).
* Lesson: It is possible to achieve expert-level performance even with very little knowledge as long as it could be compensated by search (e.g. Deep Blue computes 200 million possible moves per second for a duration of 2 minutes before choosing the "best" move)
* In NLP, search can compensate for incomplete and inaccurate knowledge.
* e.g. "take" has many meanings (take a shower, take a book, take a bus, etc.). Precise meaning can be clarified by context and by exploring all the alternatives until the meaning is unambiguous.
* This law tells us that when faced with a situation in which knowledge is yet to be acquired and codified, search is a very good way to proceed.
